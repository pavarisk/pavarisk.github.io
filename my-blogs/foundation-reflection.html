Foundation Reflection

Time to reflect on the past 5 weeks.

Three new things I've learned about myself and my ego as a result of the core learning.

  • I know that I have not been the best student for the majority of my life and I was not really too sure why. At the same time I also know that I could be better in some areas but I didn’t quite know how to start becoming better in terms of learning. The core learning in this programme has helped me understand myself better and to look at myself with less judgement and more understanding.
  • I’ve always thought being open-minded was the same as something like a growth mindset where if you keep an open mind, because it is far easier to understand something when you do not have any preconceived opinion. While there are similarities between them, those two mindsets are rather different when you actually look deeper to what a growth mindset actually is. Growth mindset is an attitude one has towards learning, where one believes that through hard work and perseverance, it is possible to keep developing your skills and talents are just a starting point to build from.
  • Lastly, I’ve found that I have known a lot of these contents have been familiar to me for a long time but I have not properly utilised them. For instance, perseverance has been something like a repeated lesson throughout my life. I have known it, seen it, used it myself but not nearly quite enough. I have given up on things in the past due to not being good at it but when I am looking back now, did I try everything I could have to get better? The simple answer is “No.” and the more complicated ones sounded a lot like excuses. Now that I understand myself better I can make the most of it and become a better version of myself.

Personal Values, Empathy and Self Awareness in learning and programming.

Personal Values, Empathy and Self Awareness are some qualities that relate to one’s Emotional Quotient (EQ) which play a massive role in their learning journey and workplace environment. Throughout foundation, there were many things I discovered about myself and my learning style. Self awareness is a very important one in my learning journey because I incorporate Growth Mindset into it and when I am looking back at myself, I see my current self as a work in progress and unbiasedly evaluate how I can improve myself as a person. To do this you must have your personal values in the right place because if you don’t value learning and see it as a waste of time, you are not going to learn since it is going to be much harder to do something when you do not want to. It is important that you have a reason to learn and to drive you to keep learning when things get hard.

Surprises throughout core learning.

I was surprised by the topics that were introduced to us throughout foundation core learning. They were all really useful to help guide us through this learning journey. The more that I practice the better I am getting, it is an amazing feeling to feel like I am progressing which this course did beautifully. Especially if I keep up with my self awareness, I can see more clearly where I have improved and which area still needs improvement.

The most challenging aspect of core learning so far.

Utilising what we have learned in our everyday life is rather difficult. Sometimes things that I learned just get put in an imaginary box in my mind forgotten about and not get utilised. Which I now realise is a huge waste, so in effort to keep learning and to be more efficient with it, I have given learning a mindful priority so that it is constantly something I think about and practice to sharpen my skills. Not in a specific area but as a skillset to be able to learn.

The awesome result of the core learning.

While the technical skills that you need and will be learning here is very important for your future jobs there is something very important that the core learning is teaching me, it is the ability to learn and how to learn effectively. With that skill set, I am not only limited to the technical skills that I have learned from the EDA but I will have the ability to learn more technical skills because I will know the “how”.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time?
Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

No way, as I have explained above. I really enjoy the core content as it teaches me to be more effective with my technical learning. If I used that time to practise programming instead it is very likely that I would have encountered a problem and get disheartened from it, if i didn’t have the problem solving skills or the mindset to keep going it is very likely that programming would have been a short lived interest I had for a while since it got too hard and I did not know what to do with it and quit. Now I have the knowledge to keep a positive mindset going and to understand that I did not fail, I haven’t succeeded YET
