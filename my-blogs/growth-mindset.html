What is Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset and how does it influence your learning?

Neuroplasticity refers to your brain’s ability to restructure or rewire itself when it recognizes the need for adaptation. In other words, it can continue developing and changing throughout life.

Growth mindset is a term used to define individuals who believe their talents can be developed (through hard work, good strategies, and input from others). People often confuse a growth mindset with being flexible or open-minded or with having a positive outlook — qualities they believe they’ve simply always had. Everyone is actually a mixture of fixed and growth mindsets, and that mixture continually evolves with experience. A “pure” growth mindset doesn’t exist, which we have to acknowledge in order to attain the benefits we seek.

This means that your learning potential is not limited by age and your brain can be further developed if we work on it. If one starts using this information wisely coupled with a growth mindset. I cannot say for certain where that person's potential would end since if you do believe in yourself and keep practicing and knowing that persistence and effort is the key to success. All the while developing your neuroplasticity, could mean that you can become a better version of yourself all together.
